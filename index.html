The Structure
2 full-body sessions per week. Every session hits every muscle group. 15 sets total each time.
Why full-body and not splits? The "2" in 2-5-15 means each muscle is trained twice a week. A chest/back split would mean chest only gets hit on upper days. Full-body sessions ensure every muscle gets its 2× weekly stimulus — and compound movements mean 3 exercises × 5 sets = 15 sets covers the whole body simultaneously.
2×
Sessions Per Week
Monday and Thursday. Each is a complete full-body workout. ~72 hrs between sessions — enough recovery, frequent enough stimulus.
5
Sets Per Muscle
Each compound movement counts toward 2–3 muscle groups at once. A thruster = 5 sets for quads AND shoulders AND triceps in one go.
15
Sets Per Session
3 exercises × 5 sets = 15. With full-body compounds that's enough to hit every major muscle. Sessions take ~30–35 minutes.
A/B
Alternate Each Week
Session A and B use different exercises for the same muscle groups — same stimulus, different movement pattern. This prevents accommodation over time.

DB Thruster (Squat → Press)
QuadsGlutesShouldersTricepsCore
5 sets8–10 repsNASM Advanced Squat
How: Dumbbells at shoulders. Full squat, then drive through the legs and use that momentum to press overhead in one fluid movement. NASM classifies this as the advanced functional squat pattern. Covers lower body AND shoulders in every rep — the single highest-value movement in this programme.
Deadlift → Upright Row
HamstringsGlutesTrapsShouldersLower Back
5 sets8–10 repsFitness Phantom
How: Hinge into a Romanian deadlift — then as you stand, pull the dumbbells straight up to chin height (upright row). Two movements chained. The hinge fires the entire posterior chain; the row hits traps and side delts. Fitness Phantom lists this as one of the highest muscle-coverage combos available with dumbbells.
DB Floor Press
ChestTricepsShoulders (front)
5 sets8–10 reps
How: Lie flat on the mat, elbows at 45°, lower until triceps touch the floor. The mat limits the range of motion which actually removes shoulder strain — no bench needed. Go heavy. This is your primary push compound, covering chest and triceps for the session.

* Back and biceps receive secondary stimulus through stabilisation during the floor press and via the hinge/row movements. If you want dedicated back volume, swap the Floor Press for the Renegade Row from the Swap Library and use the Glute Bridge + Chest Fly as your push instead.

Reverse Lunge → Bicep Curl
QuadsGlutesBicepsCore (balance)Hamstrings
5 sets8 reps each legFitness Phantom + NASM Lunge
How: Step back into a reverse lunge — as you drive back up to standing, curl the dumbbells. Reverse lunge (vs forward) is easier on the knee and better activates the glute. The curl happens at the top of the stand, using the natural momentum pause. Covers quads, glutes AND biceps per set.
Single-Leg RDL → Row
HamstringsGlutesBackCore (balance)Biceps
5 sets8 reps each legNASM Advanced Hinge + NML
How: Balance on one leg, hinge forward into a single-leg RDL — at the bottom, row the dumbbell to your hip before returning upright. NASM lists single-leg RDL as the advanced hinge pattern. Adding the row means each rep also hits the back. Go lighter than a standard RDL — balance is the challenge here.
Push-Up → T Rotation
ChestTricepsObliquesShouldersCore
5 sets6–8 reps each sideNASM Advanced Push
How: Do a push-up, then at the top rotate and reach one arm to the ceiling until your body forms a "T". Alternate the rotation side each rep. NASM lists this as their advanced functional push pattern — the rotation adds an oblique and rotational core element completely absent from the floor press in Session A. Load with a dumbbell on your back to increase difficulty.

Shoulders receive secondary stimulus from both the lunge-press and push-up. If you want dedicated shoulder sets, replace the Push-Up → T Rotation with the Arnold Press from the Swap Library (5 sets) — that brings dedicated shoulder volume while still covering triceps.

Swap Library
Rotate any exercise after 4–6 weeks. Each covers the same muscle groups as what it replaces.
When to swap: After 4–6 weeks your body adapts to the same movement pattern. Swapping in an alternative — same muscle groups, different pattern — restarts the adaptation cycle. You don't need to change everything at once; swapping one exercise per session is enough.
Clean and Press
HamstringsTrapsShouldersGlutesTriceps
5 sets6–8 repsRunstreet / Fitness Phantom
Replaces: Deadlift → Upright Row (Session A). More explosive — hinge hard, pull dumbbells to shoulders (the "clean"), then press overhead. Adds a power element missing from the RDL→row.
Renegade Row
BackBicepsCoreChest (stabilise)
5 sets6–8 each sideNASM Advanced Pull
Replaces: Floor Press (Session A) if you want to prioritise back over chest. Or add after the 3 main exercises as a 4th movement by reducing all sets to 4 (4×4 = 16, still close to cap). NASM's advanced pull-pattern functional move.
Glute Bridge + Chest Fly
GlutesChestHamstringsCore
5 sets10–12 repsFitness Phantom
Replaces: Floor Press (Session A or B). Drive hips up into a glute bridge, hold that position and perform a dumbbell chest fly. Lower body stays loaded the entire set. Entirely floor-based — one of the best mat-only upper/lower combos.
Glute Bridge + Skull Crusher
GlutesHamstringsTricepsCore
5 sets10–12 repsNML
Replaces: Push-Up → T Rotation (Session B) when you want tricep isolation alongside glute work. Hold the bridge, lower dumbbells to temples, press back. NML programme this specifically as a glute + tricep compound — both muscles loaded simultaneously throughout.
Arnold Press
Shoulders (all 3 heads)TricepsUpper Chest
5 sets10–12 repsFitness Phantom
Replaces: Any press in either session when shoulders need more direct attention. Sit cross-legged on the mat. Start palms facing you, rotate to palms facing forward as you press overhead. The rotation recruits all three delt heads — front, side, and rear — in one movement.
DB Surrender
QuadsGlutesShouldersCoreHip Flexors
5 sets6–8 each sideFitness Phantom
Replaces: Thruster (Session A) for variety. Hold dumbbells overhead throughout. Step one knee down to the mat, then the other (kneeling), then step back up. The overhead load challenges core stability and shoulders the entire time. Trains the transition between floor and standing — highly functional.
Curtsy Lunge + Rotation
Glutes (medius)QuadsObliquesAdductors
5 sets8 each sideNASM Advanced Lunge
Replaces: Reverse Lunge → Curl (Session B). Cross one leg behind into a curtsy, rotate torso toward the front leg at the bottom, then rotate back and stand. NASM's advanced lunge pattern — the only movement that trains the frontal and rotational plane, which all other exercises in this programme miss.
